      Mr. Justice McKENNA delivered the opinion of the Court.                 11       This case was argued and submitted with the Chase Case (No. 242) 256 U.S. 1, 41 Sup. Ct. 417, 65 L. Ed. ——. It is a suit by Mary Gilpin, by her next friend, to have adjudged to her a right to an allotment of lands in the Omaha Reservation, she being an Omaha Indian. The right is based on the same treaties and acts of Congress as those passed upon in the Chase Case, and the effect of the Act of May 11, 1912 (37 Stat. 111) repealing the acts—that of 1882 and that of 1893.
